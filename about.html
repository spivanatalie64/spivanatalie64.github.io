Chiseling the Square

I am a trans woman who loves exactly who I am. My journey began in Oklahoma and the foster care system—a square peg in a round hole. I didn't wait for the world to change; I chiseled away at that circle until it became a square.

For years, I lived as Johnathan Tyler Spiva. That was a life that wasn't mine—a body that was not my own, walking through existence as a prisoner to someone else's expectations. Transitioning wasn't just a change; it was a prison break into my own reality.

I am in transition, and recently, I legally became Natalie Cole-Clift Spiva. This name represents my true identity and honors every person who helped build me into who I am today.

My legal name, Natalie Cole-Clift Spiva, acknowledges every force that built me:

  • Natalie: My true name and my identity.
  • Cole: My biological mom's maiden name—I'm her twinsie! 😂
  • Clift: My father, Darren, who took me in and offered me a life of peace and dignity.
  • Spiva: Under Clift sits my adoptive father, Jeff, who shaped the person I am today and provided my foundation.

I've been in the Android and Linux development space since I was 7 years old. It started as a way to find logic in a world that had none and evolved into the expertise I use to architect systems today.

I didn't get here alone. My strength comes from my dads, Jeff and Darren, and my adoptive mother, who supports me as her "kiddo" through and through. Grounding it all is my "Hubby," Ricki, and my brother.
